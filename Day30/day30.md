# Day 30 – AI Supply Chain Builder

## Objective

The objective of this project was to understand how different supply chain decisions affect business performance. Using an AI-generated Supply Chain Builder simulator, I explored various strategies and observed their impact on cost, delivery speed, risk control, customer satisfaction, and sustainability.

---

## Company Profile

**Company Name:** Horizon Industries

* **Industry:** Organic Food
* **Core Products:** Packaged Snacks & Beverages
* **Markets Served:** USA, UK, Germany, Japan, Australia
* **Demand Pattern:** Very High & Seasonal

---

## Supply Chain Decisions

### 1. Supplier Strategy

**Selected:** Multiple Suppliers

**Reason:** Using multiple suppliers reduces supply disruption risks and provides greater flexibility during seasonal demand fluctuations.

---

### 2. Factory Location

**Selected:** Nearshore (Neighboring Region)

**Reason:** A nearshore factory provides a good balance between manufacturing cost and delivery speed while serving international markets efficiently.

---

### 3. Warehouse Strategy

**Selected:** Regional Warehouses

**Reason:** Regional warehouses help deliver products faster to customers across multiple countries and improve customer satisfaction.

---

### 4. Transportation Method

**Selected:** Road Freight

**Reason:** Road freight offers a balanced combination of flexibility, delivery speed, and transportation cost for regional distribution.

---

### 5. Inventory Strategy

**Selected:** Balanced Inventory

**Reason:** Maintaining a moderate safety stock helps handle seasonal demand while avoiding excessive inventory holding costs and product waste.

## Biggest Risk

Poor sustainability performance may increase regulatory risks and negatively affect environmentally conscious customers.

---

## Key Learnings

* Every supply chain decision involves trade-offs between cost, speed, and risk.
* Multiple suppliers improve reliability but can increase operational costs.
* Regional warehouses significantly improve delivery speed and customer satisfaction.
* Balanced inventory is suitable for businesses with seasonal demand.
* AI simulations help evaluate different business strategies before real-world implementation.
* Supply chain optimization requires balancing efficiency, customer needs, and sustainability.
